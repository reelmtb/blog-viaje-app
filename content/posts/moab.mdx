Moab – Where the Ride Becomes the Story

We didn't just plan a mountain biking trip—we chased something a little bigger. The kind of trip where the trails are legendary, the scenery feels unreal, and every moment stacks into a story you'll keep retelling.

That's how we ended up in Moab.

From the moment the tires hit that red dirt, it felt different. The traction. The silence. The scale of it all. You look around and realize quickly—this place doesn't mess around.

## Camp Life – The Basecamp Vibe

We rolled into the Sand Flats Recreation Area with trucks full of bikes, gear, and just enough snacks to pretend we packed responsibly. Campsites stretched out ahead of us—some tucked closer to town, others climbing toward the rugged backdrop of the La Sal Mountains.

We picked a spot that gave us the best of both worlds: close enough for convenience, far enough to feel like we were out there. Nights came with that desert stillness—no city noise, just wind, laughter, and the occasional "remember that line?" replayed around camp.

![Camp setup in Sand Flats Recreation Area](/images/moab2.jpeg)

A few days later, we switched it up and moved to Goose Island Campground, right along the Colorado River. Completely different vibe. The sound of the river replaced the silence, and the canyon walls lit up at sunset like they were putting on a show just for us.

And then came one of those unexpected highlights—the kind that sticks with you just as much as the riding. One of our buddies stepped up and turned camp into a full-on taco spot. Carne asada sizzling, tortillas warming, everyone hovering nearby pretending not to be starving. After a full day on the bike, those tacos didn't just hit—they landed. Easily one of the best meals of the trip, no question.

## The Rides – Earning Every Mile

We came to ride, and Moab delivered.

![Mountain biking action on Moab trails](/images/moab3.jpeg)

### HyMasa / Captain Ahab – The Test

The day we tackled Captain Ahab Trail, the weather felt perfect—mid 60s, blue skies. But that illusion didn't last long.

The climb started, and it didn't ease you in. It demanded focus right away—technical moves, ledges, line choices that punish hesitation. Every pedal stroke felt earned. You'd clear a section, catch your breath, look up… and realize there was more waiting.

And yet, somehow, you keep going.

At the top, the world opens up. The La Sal Mountains stand tall in the distance, like a reminder of how small you are out there. It's one of those moments where nobody says much—you just take it in.

Then comes the descent.

Fast. Technical. Relentless. Drops appear out of nowhere, followed by sections that demand precision. Just when you think you're flowing, a punchy climb reminds you this trail isn't done with you yet. And near the end, those exposed sections keep you honest. It's the kind of ride that leaves your legs smoked and your mind buzzing.

### Porcupine Rim – The Experience

Riding Porcupine Rim Trail felt different from the start.

Less urgency. More presence.

We rode as a group—stopping often, taking photos, laughing about lines we almost took (and a few we definitely shouldn't have). Then came The Snotch. We rolled up, looked at it, sized it up… and collectively decided we liked our current health status. Still, the rest of the trail gave us plenty to work with—drops, rock rolls, and that signature Moab chunk that keeps you locked in.

And then, near the end, the trail opens up alongside the Colorado River.

That stretch? It's something else.

You're tired. Your hands are buzzing. But the views pull you forward. Big canyon walls, the river cutting through it all, and that feeling that you're exactly where you're supposed to be.

## The Simple Stuff That Makes It

Mornings started slow at Moab Coffee Roasters—coffee in hand, talking through the day's ride like we hadn't already overhyped it the night before.

Evenings ended with full plates at Antica Forma or bouncing between options at the Moab Food Truck Park. But honestly, nothing beat that night at camp with the carne asada tacos.

Because trips like this aren't just about the trails.

They're about the in-between moments—the laughs, the meals, the shared "did that really just happen?" kind of memories.



## Why Moab Stays With You

Moab doesn't just give you great riding—it gives you stories.

The kind you replay on the drive home.
The kind that make you start planning the next trip before this one's even over.

We came for the trails.
But what we left with… was way more than that.

## What to Pack – Don’t Learn This the Hard Way

Moab has a way of rewarding preparation… and punishing shortcuts.

We all showed up thinking we had what we needed—and by day one, we knew exactly what we were glad we brought (and what we wished we had).

On the bike, this isn’t your average ride. The desert is dry, the terrain is unforgiving, and the effort adds up fast. Bring more water than you think you need—you’ll drink all of it. Electrolytes are key, especially when the sun starts reflecting off the rock. A solid repair kit isn’t optional out here either: spare tube, tire plugs, pump or CO2, and a multi-tool. And don’t forget snacks—this place burns energy quicker than expected.

Gear-wise, Moab demands respect. Tires with strong sidewalls make a difference on sharp rock edges. A helmet is a given, but knee and elbow pads are worth it once you start committing to lines. Gloves help keep your hands fresh on long descents, and sunglasses are essential for both sun and dust.

At camp, the little things go a long way. Desert days are warm, but nights cool off fast—bring layers. A stove or grill turns a good evening into a great one (especially if someone’s cooking up carne asada tacos). Headlamps, lanterns, and a well-stocked cooler all add to the experience.

And maybe the most underrated thing to pack: a little extra energy for the post-ride hangs. That’s where the trip really comes together.

## Conclusion – More Than Just a Trip

Moab isn’t just a place you visit—it’s a place you feel.

It’s in the grind of a technical climb when your legs start questioning your decisions.
It’s in the quiet moments at the top, taking in the endless red rock and distant peaks.
It’s in the rush of a descent where everything clicks, even if just for a few seconds.
And it’s in the laughter back at camp, replaying every near miss and every clean line.

We came for the riding.
We stayed for the experience.

And somewhere between the trails, the river, and those late-night tacos under the stars…
Moab became more than a trip.

It became a story we’re already looking forward to continuing.
